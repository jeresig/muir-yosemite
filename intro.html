Scottish-born American naturalist John Muir (1838-1914) was instrumental in the preservation of wilderness in the United States, including Yosemite and Sequoia National Parks. He was also a prolific author and published accounts of his adventures trekking through the Sierra Nevada.
Photo by Carleton Watkins, 1878-1881. Source
Through his advocacy and teaching, whether he was publishing essays, greeting tourists to Yosemite Valley, or taking President Theodore Roosevelt on a life-changing camping trip, John Muir helped the public to understand and appreciate both the scientific workings and the spiritual glory of the natural world around them.
Now you can explore today's Yosemite with Muir as your guide. Use the "Map" button above to visit one of several locations in Yosemite National Park or the neighboring Sequoia National Park.
To get started, open the map and select "Range of Light." Then sit back, relax, and breath in the fresh Sierra Nevada air.
