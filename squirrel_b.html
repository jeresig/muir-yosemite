The Douglas Squirrel

"Go where you will throughout the noble woods of the Sierra Nevada, among the giant pines and spruces of the lower zones, up through the towering Silver Firs to the storm-bent thickets of the summit peaks, you everywhere find this little squirrel the master-existence."
Photo by Robin Horn. Source
"Though only a few inches long, so intense is his fiery vigor and restlessness, he stirs every grove with wild life, and makes himself more important than even the huge bears that shuffle through the tangled underbrush beneath him."
"Every wind is fretted by his voice, almost every bole and branch feels the sting of his sharp feet."
Photo by Jesse Taylor. Source
"From the nose to the root of the tail he measures about eight inches; and his tail, which he so effectively uses in interpreting his feelings, is about six inches in length."
"He has long black whiskers, which gives him a rather fierce look when observed closely, strong claws, sharp as fish-hooks, and the brightest of bright eyes, full of telling speculation."
Photo by LDELD. Source
"I cannot begin to tell here how much he has cheered my lonely wanderings during all the years I have been pursuing my studies in these glorious wilds; or how much unmistakable humanity I have found in him."
Source: The Mountains of California, Chapter 9.
Photo by Walter Siegmund. Source
A douglas squirrel collects bark for its nest in Mariposa Grove, Yosemite.
