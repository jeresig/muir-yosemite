Scottish-born American naturalist John Muir (1838-1914) was instrumental in the preservation of wilderness in the United States, including Yosemite and Sequoia National Parks. He was also a prolific author and published accounts of his adventures trekking through the Sierra Nevada.
Photo by Carleton Watkins, 1878-1881. Source
Through his advocacy and teaching, whether he was publishing essays, greeting tourists to Yosemite Valley, or taking President Theodore Roosevelt on a life-changing camping trip, John Muir helped people to understand and appreciate both the scientific workings and the spiritual power of the natural world around them.
Photo by Carleton Watkins, 1878-1881. Source
